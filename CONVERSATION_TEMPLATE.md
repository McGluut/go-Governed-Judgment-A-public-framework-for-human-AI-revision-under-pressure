## Notes

- Leave a field blank rather than forcing a bad answer.
- Use `Guidance field:` to capture the active relation, not only one pole's wording.
- Use `Live tension to preserve:` to keep productive contradiction alive rather than flatten it into harmony.
- Use `Constraint to suspend for re-entry:` when drag blocks movement but outright deletion is not yet justified.
- Use `Where I may be inconsistent:` to mark uncertainty without treating it as failure.
- Use `One next step:` and `Review point:` to make restart operational rather than diagnostic only.
- Use `Terms you may redefine:` to grant controlled relabeling without collapsing judgment.
