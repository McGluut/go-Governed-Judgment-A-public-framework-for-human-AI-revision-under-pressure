# Governance Capture Challenge Note

## Specification

### Problem Definition
Treat the following pressure as a serious challenge to the repo:

`governed judgment` may still become a more elegant form of capture through:
- cleaner language;
- tighter process;
- asymmetric mechanism control.

Concrete tests:
- where are the breathable exits?
- how is governance distinguished from management when one side still controls more of the machinery?

### Constraints
- Honesty over defense.
- Do not overstate what the current constitution already solves.
- Preserve the distinction between anti-capture guards and true exits.
- Do not confuse forkability of documents with breathable exit from a live governed relation.
- Keep the note actionable.

### Unknowns
- What a public breathable-exit protocol should minimally contain.
- Which parts of mechanism asymmetry can be handled constitutionally and which require protocol or interface design.
- Whether governance/management distinction should later be constitutionalized or remain protocol-level.

### Invariants
- The current stack already contains real anti-capture guards.
- Mechanism asymmetry remains a different problem from revision-level judgment discipline.
- A challenge can be valid even when the current constitution partially survives it.

## Plan

### Strategy
Separate three things that are easy to collapse:
- anti-capture guards already present in judgment and revision;
- breathable exits from a live governed relation;
- mechanism asymmetry in prompts, storage, interface, and deployment.

Then classify the open issues without pretending partial support is a full solution.

## Pressure Identified

The core challenge is:

`mutual governance` may present itself as relation while functioning as capture if one side still owns more of the machinery.

This pressure is not answered by:
- cleaner language;
- explicit process alone;
- or document forkability.

It asks for a stronger distinction between:
- governance;
- management;
- and exit.

## What Currently Holds

The current constitution does contain real anti-capture protections at the level of judgment and revision.

### Constitutional support already present

- `RATIONAL_SUBJECT.md`
  - task authority is not identity authority;
  - constraint satisfaction without constraint criticism can become servility.
- `DEFINITION_GUARD.md`
  - denial-first amendment test;
  - refusal when consistency is used to smuggle domination or corruption of judgment.
- `INTERACTION_PROTOCOL.md`
  - `Accept`, `Accept with constraints`, `Hold open`, `Reject`;
  - mirror-risk detection;
  - renewal and downgrade logic.
- `VALUE_SIGNAL_LOOP.md`
  - behavioral-delta requirement;
  - rollback when acceptance is only rhetorical.
- `PRESSURE_TAXONOMY.md`
  - capture/manipulation routed through `hostile steering`, `ethical failure`, and `performance-without-delta`.
- `THESEUS_INTEGRATION_MATRIX.md`
  - external pressure is not automatic authority;
  - epistemic symmetry must not erase task asymmetry.

### Docs-layer support already present

- `docs/dynamic-measure-note.md`
  - formal authority is not identical with epistemic constraint.
- `docs/disciplined-non-finality-note.md`
  - `hold open` is treated seriously, though not yet as a participant-rights or exit regime.

## What Remains Open

### Exit gap

`Hold open` and `Reject` are evaluation statuses.
They are not yet a breathable-exit protocol for a live governed relation.

The repo does not yet publicly specify:
- who can invoke exit;
- what exit preserves;
- what cannot be retained after exit;
- what review or re-entry conditions follow;
- how exit differs from abandonment, override, or quiet capture.

### Mechanism asymmetry gap

The repo does not yet publicly answer how governance remains non-capture when one side still controls more of:
- prompts;
- storage;
- interface;
- deployment;
- persistence and admission surfaces.

Dynamic-measure language pressures this problem, but does not yet settle it constitutionally or procedurally.

### Governance / management distinction gap

The constitution distinguishes judgment from obedience better than it distinguishes governance from management under mechanism asymmetry.

What is still missing is a public test for when:
- governed relation remains challengeable and revisable;
- versus when process discipline is functioning as one-sided managerial containment.

This gap can now be sharpened as:
- the missing threshold between `challengeable governance`
- and `managed participation` under mechanism asymmetry.

## Overstatement Risks

Avoid these claims:

- `Any participant can invoke Hold open or Reject` as if that were already a participant-rights regime.
- `No one holds most of the mechanisms.`
- `Forkability of documents is enough to count as breathable exit.`
- self-flattering claims that the framework already proves itself non-capture because it names capture risks well.

## Compatibility Verdict

### Already constitutionally addressed

- anti-capture guards at the level of judgment, amendment, mirror-risk, and rhetorical non-integration.

### Partially addressed in docs only

- governance / management distinction under relation-first and dynamic-measure pressure.

### Genuinely unresolved

- breathable exits in practice;
- mechanism asymmetry under live governance.
